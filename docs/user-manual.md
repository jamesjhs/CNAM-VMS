## 1. What Is the VMS?

The Volunteer Management System is a private website for use by City of Norwich Aviation Museum volunteers and staff. It is designed to be your central hub for:

- **Signing in and out** of shifts and schedules
- **Seeing your tasks** — what you have been asked to do and when
- **Reading announcements** from the museum team
- **Sharing documents** such as policies, guides, and resources
- **Managing volunteers** (for administrators) — approving new members, assigning roles, and reviewing activity

Everything is kept secure — only people who have been given access can sign in, and each person only sees the areas they are permitted to use.

---

## 2. Getting Access

To use the VMS, you need to be added to the system by an administrator. The system does not have a self-service registration form at the moment, so if you are a new volunteer who needs access, please get in touch with your museum contact or team leader and ask them to set up your account.

Once your account has been created and approved, you will be able to sign in using your email address. No password is needed — see the next section for how that works.

---

## 3. Signing In

The VMS uses a sign-in method called a **magic link**. This means you do not need to remember a password. Instead, every time you want to sign in, the system sends a special link to your email address, and clicking that link signs you straight in.

### How to sign in, step by step

1. Open your web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, or Safari all work fine) and go to the VMS address — your administrator will have given you this.

2. You will see the sign-in page. It looks like this:

   > **CNAM Volunteer Management** — *City of Norwich Aviation Museum*
   >
   > **Sign in**
   > Enter your email address and we'll send you a magic link.
   >
   > [ Your email address ] [ Send magic link ]

3. Type in your email address and click **Send magic link**.

4. You will see a confirmation page saying "Check your email". Check your email inbox — you should receive an email from the museum within a minute or two. The email will have a subject line along the lines of *"Sign in to CNAM VMS"*.

   > **Can't find the email?** Check your junk or spam folder. If it is still not there after five minutes, go back to the sign-in page and try again.

5. Click the link in the email. It will open the VMS in your browser and sign you in automatically. You do not need to do anything else.

   > **Important:** The link in the email works only once and expires after 24 hours. If you click it and it says the link is invalid, simply go back to the sign-in page and request a new one.

6. You will be taken to your personal Dashboard (see Section 5).

## 4. The Home Page

When you visit the VMS without being signed in, you will see the home page. This is a welcome page for the museum's Volunteer Management System. It has a brief description of what the system does and a **"Sign in with Email"** button.

If you are already signed in, the home page shows two buttons instead: one to go to your **Dashboard**, and one to go to the **Admin Panel** (if you have administrator access).

The navigation bar at the top of every page shows:
- **CNAM VMS** (in the top-left) — clicking this always takes you back to the home page
- **Sign in** or your email address and a **Sign out** button (in the top-right)
- Links to **Dashboard**, **Admin**, and **Upload** (these only appear when you are signed in)

## 5. Your Dashboard

Once you have signed in, you will be taken to your personal Dashboard. This is your starting point in the VMS — think of it as your noticeboard.

The dashboard greets you by name (if your name has been set up) and shows three main areas:

### My Tasks

This area will show you the tasks that have been assigned to you. Tasks might include things like:

- Helping on a particular day at the museum
- Completing a training activity
- Preparing materials for an event

> **Coming soon:** Full task management functionality — seeing your tasks, marking them complete, and adding notes — is currently being developed and will be added to the system shortly.

### Schedule

This area will show your upcoming shifts and any dates you have indicated you are available to volunteer.

> **Coming soon:** The scheduling calendar is under development.

### Announcements

This area will show the latest news and messages from the museum team — things like upcoming events, changes to procedures, or general updates.

> **Coming soon:** The announcements board is under development.

---

### Account Pending Notice

If you have just been given access to the system but your account has not yet been fully approved, you may see a yellow notice at the top of your dashboard that says:

> **Account Pending:** Your account is awaiting approval. Some features may be limited.

If you see this, your account is in the system but an administrator has not yet marked it as active. Please contact your team leader or the museum administrator and they will be able to activate your account.

---

## 6. Signing Out

To sign out of the VMS, click your email address in the top-right corner of any page, and then click **Sign out**. You will be returned to the home page.

It is good practice to sign out when you have finished, especially if you are using a shared computer.

If you forget to sign out, your session will expire automatically after a period of inactivity.

---

## 7. Your Account Status

Every account in the VMS has one of three statuses:

| Status | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Pending** | Your account has been created but is waiting for an administrator to approve it. You can sign in, but some features may be unavailable. |
| **Active** | Your account is fully approved and you have access to all the areas you have been given permission for. |
| **Suspended** | Your account has been temporarily or permanently disabled. You will not be able to sign in. If you believe this is a mistake, contact your administrator. |

If your account has been suspended, you will see an error message when you try to sign in. Please get in touch with your museum contact.

## 8. Uploading Files (Administrators)

If you have administrator access, you can upload files to the VMS to share with the team. This might include documents such as:

- Risk assessments
- Volunteer handbooks
- Event briefings
- Procedures and policies
- Photographs

### How to upload a file

1. Sign in and click **Upload** in the navigation bar at the top of the page.
2. Click **Select file** and choose the file you want to upload from your computer.
3. Click **Upload File**.
4. If the upload is successful, you will see a green confirmation message with the file name and size.
5. If something goes wrong (for example, the file is too large or the wrong type), a red message will explain what the problem is.

### File restrictions

The following types of file can be uploaded:

- **Images:** JPEG, PNG, GIF, WebP
- **Documents:** PDF, Word (.docx), Excel (.xlsx)
- **Data files:** CSV, plain text (.txt)

Files must be no larger than **10 MB** (your administrator may have set a different limit).

Files that do not match these types will be rejected automatically. If you need to upload a file that is not on this list, please speak to your administrator.

> **Where are the uploaded files?** Once uploaded, files are stored securely on the server. A library page where you can browse and download uploaded files is currently under development.

## 9. The Admin Panel (Administrators)

The Admin Panel is available to administrators with appropriate permissions. To access it, click **Admin** in the navigation bar.

> If you do not have permission to view the Admin Panel, you will see an "Access Denied" message. Contact your system administrator if you believe you should have access.

### What the Admin Panel shows

The Admin Panel is the system overview page. It currently shows:

#### Your Account Details

A summary of your own account, including:

- Your **email address**
- Your **name**
- Your **account status** (Active, Pending, or Suspended)
- The **capabilities** (permissions) your account has — these are shown as small labels such as `admin:users.read` and `admin:files.write`

#### System Statistics

Three summary figures:

- **Total Users** — how many people have accounts in the system
- **Roles** — how many roles are defined (a role is a named set of permissions, such as "Root" or "Volunteer")
- **Audit Events** — how many actions have been recorded in the audit log

#### Recent Audit Log

A table showing the ten most recent actions in the system. Each row shows:

- **When** — the date and time of the action (shown in UK format: day/month/year)
- **User** — who performed the action (or "system" if it was an automatic action)
- **Action** — a code describing what happened (for example, `USER_SIGNIN` means someone signed in, `USER_CREATED` means a new account was created, `FILE_UPLOADED` means a file was uploaded)
- **Resource** — what type of record was affected (for example, "User" or "FileAsset")

The audit log is a complete, permanent record of everything significant that happens in the system. It is useful for spotting problems, reviewing who has been active, and maintaining accountability.

---

### Roles and Permissions

The system uses a permissions model based on **roles** and **capabilities**:

- A **capability** is a specific permission, such as the ability to view all users or to upload files.
- A **role** is a named collection of capabilities.
- Each user can be assigned one or more roles.

Two roles are created automatically when the system is first set up:

| Role | Who it is for | What it allows |
|---|---|---|
| **Root** | The lead administrator | Everything — full access to all features |
| **Volunteer** | Standard volunteers | Viewing tasks (once task management is implemented) |

> **Note:** Full role and user management screens — where you can add users, approve accounts, assign roles, and create new roles — are currently being developed and will be added to the Admin Panel in a future update.

## 10. Email Notifications

The VMS can send email notifications to volunteers automatically. Currently, the system is set up to send notifications for the following events:

| Notification | When it is sent |
|---|---|
| **Sign-in link** | Whenever you request to sign in |
| **Task assigned** | When a task is assigned to you *(coming with task management)* |
| **Task overdue** | When a task you have been assigned has passed its due date *(coming with task management)* |
| **Announcement** | When a new announcement is posted *(coming with announcements)* |

All emails come from the museum's VMS email address and will have "CNAM VMS" in the sender name. If you are not receiving emails, check your junk or spam folder and, if necessary, add the sender address to your contacts.

## 11. Privacy and Security

The VMS is designed with privacy and security in mind:

- **No passwords are stored.** The system uses magic links (one-time email links) to sign you in. There is no password database that could be stolen.
- **Your email address is kept private.** It is only visible to administrators and is stored securely.
- **Everything is logged.** Every sign-in and every significant action is recorded in the audit log, so any unusual activity can be spotted quickly.
- **Access is controlled.** Each person can only see and do what they have been given permission for. Trying to access a page you do not have permission for will show an "Access Denied" message rather than any sensitive information.
- **File uploads are checked.** Only certain types of file can be uploaded, and the system checks both the file's extension and its actual content type to prevent unsafe files from being stored.
- **The system is kept up to date.** Security updates to the underlying software are applied regularly.

If you notice anything unusual or suspect that your account has been accessed without your knowledge, please contact your administrator immediately.

## 12. Frequently Asked Questions

**I typed my email address but the sign-in email never arrived. What do I do?**

First, check your junk or spam folder — sign-in emails are sometimes filtered there. If it is not there after five minutes, go back to the sign-in page and try again. Make sure you are using the email address that was set up with your account. If problems continue, contact your administrator.

---

**The link in my email said it was invalid or had expired. What do I do?**

Magic links can only be used once and expire after 24 hours. Go back to the sign-in page, enter your email address, and request a new link.

---

**I can sign in but it says my account is "Pending". What does that mean?**

Your account has been created but has not yet been approved by an administrator. Contact your team leader or museum administrator and ask them to activate your account.

---

**It says "Access Denied" when I try to open a page. What do I do?**

This means you do not have permission to view that page. If you believe you should have access, contact your administrator and let them know which page you were trying to reach.

---

**How do I change my name or email address?**

At the moment, these can only be changed by an administrator. A profile editing page is planned for a future update. In the meantime, contact your administrator.

---

**Can I use the VMS on my phone?**

Yes. The VMS works in the web browser on any smartphone or tablet. Open your phone's browser, go to the VMS address, and sign in as normal.

---

**I have forgotten the web address for the VMS. Where can I find it?**

Your administrator will be able to give you the address. It will usually start with `https://` followed by the museum's domain name.

---

**Can I stay signed in permanently?**

Your session will remain active for as long as you are using the system. If you close the browser or do not use the system for a period of time, you will be signed out automatically and will need to request a new sign-in link. This is a security feature.

---

**Something is broken or not working as I expect. Who do I tell?**

Please contact your administrator and describe what you were doing, what you expected to happen, and what actually happened. If you see any error messages, note them down or take a screenshot — this will help the administrator (or the technical team) to fix the problem more quickly.
