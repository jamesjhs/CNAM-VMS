## 1. What Is the VMS?

The Volunteer Management System is a private website for use by City of Norwich Aviation Museum volunteers and staff. It is designed to be your central hub for:

- **Signing in securely** using your email address and password
- **Seeing the schedule** — upcoming events and shifts you can sign up for
- **Reading announcements** from the museum team
- **Working with your team** — seeing your team's active tasks and recording work you have done
- **Sharing documents** such as policies, guides, and resources
- **Managing volunteers** (for administrators) — approving new members, assigning roles, and reviewing activity

Everything is kept secure — only people who have been given access can sign in, and each person only sees the areas they are permitted to use.

---

## 2. Getting Access

To use the VMS, you need to be added to the system by an administrator. The system does not have a self-service registration form, so if you are a new volunteer who needs access, please get in touch with your museum contact or team leader and ask them to set up your account.

Once your account has been created, you will receive an email from the administrator with your initial sign-in details. You will be prompted to set your own password the first time you sign in.

## 3. Signing In

Signing in to the VMS is a **two-step process** to keep your account secure:

```
┌─────────────────────┐
│  STEP 1: Password   │
│  (You → VMS)        │
│  Email + Password   │
└────────────┬────────┘
             │
             ✓ Verified
             │
┌────────────▼────────┐
│  STEP 2: OTP Code   │
│  (Email → You)      │
│  6-digit code       │
└────────────┬────────┘
             │
             ✓ Verified
             │
┌────────────▼────────┐
│   Dashboard 🎉      │
│  Welcome & Home     │
└─────────────────────┘
```

### Step-by-step sign-in guide

#### 1️⃣ Start at the sign-in page

Open your web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, or Safari all work fine) and go to the VMS address — your administrator will have given you this.

You will see a form with fields for **Email address** and **Password**.

#### 2️⃣ Enter your credentials

- Type your **email address** (exactly as it was set up in the system)
- Type your **password** (case-sensitive)

**Tip:** There is a checkbox **"Keep me signed in for 7 days"**. 
- ✅ Tick this on **your own computer** to avoid re-signing-in frequently
- ❌ Do NOT tick this on a **shared or public computer**

#### 3️⃣ Check your email for the verification code

Click **Sign in** and the system will immediately send a **6-digit verification code** to your email address. You will be taken to a code entry screen.

Check your email inbox — the code usually arrives within a minute or two.

> **Can't find the email?** 
> - Check your Junk or Spam folder
> - The code is only valid for **10 minutes**
> - If it expires, go back and click "Request a new code"
> - After 5 failed attempts, you must start over (for security)

#### 4️⃣ Enter the verification code

Type the 6-digit code into the box on the verification page and click **Verify**.

#### 5️⃣ You're in! 🎉

Once verified, you will be taken to your **Dashboard** (see [Section 5](#5-your-dashboard)).

> **First sign-in?** If an administrator has set your account to require a password change, you will see a password-setting page first. See [Section 14](#14-managing-your-password) for details.

---

### Forgot your password?

If you have forgotten your password, click **Forgot password?** on the sign-in page.

**Password reset process:**

1. Enter your email address
2. A password reset link is sent to your email (expires in **24 hours**)
3. Click the link and follow the instructions to set a new password
4. Sign in with your new password

---

### Troubleshooting sign-in

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| **"Invalid email or password"** | Check you entered them exactly correctly (passwords are case-sensitive). Try resetting your password. |
| **"Account suspended"** | Your account has been disabled. Contact your administrator. |
| **"Account pending"** | Your account is awaiting approval. Contact your team leader or administrator. |
| **"Verification code invalid"** | The code may have expired. Click **Back** and try signing in again. |
| **"Too many failed attempts"** | For security, the system has locked the sign-in. Go back and request a new code. |

## 4. The Home Page

When you visit the VMS without being signed in, you will see the home page. This is a welcome page for the museum's Volunteer Management System. It has a brief description of what the system does and a **"Sign in"** button.

If you are already signed in, the home page shows buttons to go to your **Dashboard** and to the **Admin Panel** (if you have administrator access).

The navigation bar at the top of every page shows:
- **CNAM VMS** (in the top-left) — clicking this always takes you back to the home page
- Links to **Dashboard**, **Schedule**, **Announcements**, **Teams**, and **Files** (these appear when you are signed in)
- Your name in the top-right, which links to your **Profile** page, along with a **Sign out** button
- An **Admin** menu (if you have administrator access) with links to the various admin sections

## 5. Your Dashboard

Once you have signed in, you will be taken to your personal Dashboard. This is your starting point in the VMS — think of it as your noticeboard.

The dashboard greets you by name (if your name has been set up) and shows the following areas:

### Upcoming Events

A list of the next events and scheduled shifts in the coming 30 days, showing the event title, date, and whether you have signed up. Click **View schedule** to see the full calendar.

### Recent Announcements

A preview of the three most recent announcements from the museum team — things like upcoming events, changes to procedures, or general updates. Click **View all announcements** to read everything.

### My Availability

A quick link to record your general volunteering preferences, or to update specific dates when you are available. See [Section 9](#9-the-schedule--calendar) and [Section 11](#11-your-availability-preferences) for more detail.

### My Profile

A link to your personal profile page where you can update your name and contact details.

---

### Account Pending Notice

If your account has not yet been fully approved, you may see a yellow notice at the top of your dashboard:

> **Account Pending:** Your account is awaiting approval. Some features may be limited.

If you see this, contact your team leader or the museum administrator and they will be able to activate your account.

## 6. Signing Out

To sign out of the VMS, click **Sign out** in the top-right corner of any page. You will be returned to the home page.

It is good practice to sign out when you have finished, especially if you are using a shared computer.

If you did not tick "Keep me signed in", your session will expire automatically after **two hours** of inactivity, and you will need to sign in again. If you did tick it, your session lasts for **seven days**.

## 7. Your Account Status

Every account in the VMS has one of three statuses:

| Status | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Pending** | Your account has been created but is waiting for an administrator to approve it. You can sign in, but some features may be unavailable. |
| **Active** | Your account is fully approved and you have access to all the areas you have been given permission for. |
| **Suspended** | Your account has been temporarily or permanently disabled. You will not be able to sign in. If you believe this is a mistake, contact your administrator. |

If your account has been suspended, you will see an error message when you try to sign in. Please get in touch with your museum contact.

## 8. Announcements

The Announcements page shows news and messages from the museum team. To reach it, click **Announcements** in the navigation bar.

Announcements are listed with the newest first. Any announcement marked as **Pinned** appears at the top of the list and is highlighted — pinned announcements contain important or time-sensitive information.

Each announcement shows the title, the message text, the name of the person who posted it, and the date it was posted.

## 9. The Schedule & Calendar

The schedule page shows the museum's calendar for the current month. To reach it, click **Schedule** in the navigation bar.

### Browsing the calendar

The calendar shows each day of the month in a grid. Days that have events or scheduled shifts show small coloured labels. Days where you have recorded your availability show a green dot.

- **Navigate between months** using the **◀ Previous** and **Next ▶** buttons at the top.
- **Click on any day** to see the details for that day in a panel below the calendar.

Event labels are colour-coded by type:

| Colour | What it means |
|---|---|
| Blue | A museum event |
| Purple | A rostered shift that needs volunteers |
| Amber | Help needed |

A **✓** tick on an event label means you have already signed up for it.

### Signing up for events

When you click a day in the panel below the calendar, you will see a list of events and shifts for that day. For each one you can:

- Click **Sign up** to add yourself — the system will check whether there is still space.
- Click **Withdraw** to remove your sign-up if your plans change.

If an event has reached its maximum number of volunteers, the Sign up button will be disabled.

### Recording your availability for a day

In the day detail panel, there is also an **Availability** section where you can tell the museum when you are free on that particular date:

1. Enter a **from** and **until** time (or leave blank if you are available all day).
2. Tick the **duties** you are willing to help with on that day — for example, Aircraft Guide, Tearoom Helper, or Grass Cutting.
3. Add any **notes** (optional).
4. Click **Save availability**.

To remove your availability for a day, click **Remove**.

### Rolling duties

At the bottom of the schedule page is a **Rolling duties** panel listing duties that are always available — things like grass cutting or interior cleaning that happen regularly regardless of specific events. These are shown for reference so you know what you can volunteer for at any time.

## 10. Teams

Volunteers are organised into teams. To see the teams and their current work, click **Teams** in the navigation bar.

The Teams page lists all teams alphabetically. For each team you can see:

- The **team name** and a short description
- The **team leader** (name and email address)
- How many **members** are in the team
- Any **active tasks** — work that the team is currently undertaking

### Active tasks

Each active task shows:
- The **task title** and a brief description
- The **urgency level** — Urgent (red), Moderate (amber), or Routine (green)
- The **task type** — for example, maintenance, inspection, or restoration work
- How many people are needed and whether a supervisor is required

Click on a team name to go to that team's individual page, where you can:
- See full details of each active task, including equipment needed and any safety notes
- **Submit a work log** — record what you did, how long it took, and any observations
- **Add feedback** on a task

## 11. Your Availability Preferences

As well as recording availability for specific dates (see [Section 9](#9-the-schedule--calendar)), you can set your general volunteering preferences — the types of activity you are interested in helping with.

To update your preferences, go to your **Dashboard** and click **My availability**, or visit **Profile → Activity preferences**.

Tick the activities you are happy to volunteer for:

- Site Works (general maintenance and site improvements)
- Groundskeeping (grounds and green spaces)
- Aircraft Maintenance (restoration and maintenance assistance)
- Shop (museum gift shop)
- Tearoom (café and tearoom service)
- Aircraft Guide (guiding visitors around the aircraft)
- Museum Front of House (greeting visitors at the entrance)

You can also add a **note** — for example, to mention particular skills, days you are generally available, or anything else the museum team would find helpful.

Click **Save preferences** when you are done.

## 12. Your Profile

Your profile page lets you view and update your personal account information. To reach it, click your name in the top-right corner of any page.

### What you can see

- Your **email address** (read-only — contact an administrator to change this)
- Your **account status** (Active, Pending, or Suspended)
- Your **account type** (Volunteer, Staff, or Member)
- The date you **joined** the system

### What you can update

- **Display name** — the name shown throughout the VMS. Click **Save** after changing it.
- **Telephone numbers** — you can add one or more phone numbers with optional labels (for example, "Mobile" or "Home"). Click **Add number** to save a new one. Click **Remove** next to a number to delete it.

### Roles and teams

Your profile also shows the **roles** and **teams** you have been assigned to. These are managed by administrators — contact your administrator if these need updating.

### Permissions

At the bottom of your profile page is a list of your **capabilities** — the specific actions you are permitted to perform in the system. These are granted via your roles and are for information only.

### Danger Zone — Deleting Your Account

> ⚠️ **This action is permanent and cannot be reversed.**

At the very bottom of your profile page, you will find the **Danger Zone** section. From here you can permanently delete your own account.

Clicking **Delete my account** expands a confirmation form. You must enter your current password to proceed. Once confirmed:

- Your account and **all associated data** (availability records, event sign-ups, team memberships, phone numbers) will be permanently removed.
- You will be signed out immediately.
- Your information will not be recoverable.

If you are unsure, click **Cancel** to dismiss the form without taking any action.

> **Note for administrators:** You cannot delete your own account from the admin panel. The self-deletion option in your own Profile page is the only way to do this. The root administrator account cannot be self-deleted either — contact your system manager if the root account needs to be removed.

## 14. Managing Your Password

### Setting your password for the first time

When an administrator creates your account, they may set a temporary password and flag your account to require a password change on first sign-in. After you enter your email and temporary password and verify your identity, you will be taken to the **Set your password** page before you can access the rest of the system.

Enter your temporary password, choose a new password (at least 8 characters), confirm it, and click **Set password & continue**.

### Changing your password voluntarily

To change your password at any time, go to your **Profile** page and look for the **Change password** option, or go directly to the change-password page. You will need to enter your current password, then choose and confirm a new one.

### Resetting a forgotten password

If you have forgotten your password:

1. Go to the sign-in page and click **Forgot password?**
2. Enter your email address and click **Send reset link**.
3. Check your email — you will receive a password reset link within a few minutes.
4. Click the link and enter a new password. The link expires after **24 hours**.
5. Once reset, sign in with your new password as normal.

## 15. Uploading Files (Administrators)

If you have the appropriate administrator permission (`admin:files.write`), you can upload files to the VMS. To reach the upload page, click **Admin → Upload** in the navigation bar.

### How to upload a file

1. Click **Select file** and choose the file from your computer.
2. Click **Upload File**.
3. If the upload is successful, you will see a green confirmation message with the file name and size.
4. If something goes wrong (for example, the file is too large or the wrong type), a red message will explain the problem.

### File restrictions

The following types of file can be uploaded:

- **Images:** JPEG, PNG, GIF, WebP
- **Documents:** PDF, Word (.docx), Excel (.xlsx)
- **Data files:** CSV, plain text (.txt)

Files must be no larger than **10 MB** (your administrator may have configured a different limit).

Once uploaded, files appear immediately in the File Library (see [Section 13](#13-file-library)) for all signed-in users to see and download. Administrators with the `admin:files.read` or `admin:files.write` capability can also manage files from the **Admin → Files** page, where they can delete files that are no longer needed.

### User Management

**Required permission:** `admin:users.read` (to view), `admin:users.write` (to make changes)

Access via **Admin → Users**.

The Users page lists every account in the system with their name, email, account type (Volunteer, Staff, or Member), status, assigned roles, and team memberships.

#### Adding a new user

Fill in the **Add user** form at the top of the page:

1. Enter the new user's **email address** (required).
2. Enter their **full name** (optional — they can add this themselves later).
3. Select their **account type**: Volunteer, Staff, or Member.
4. Click **Add user**.

The new account is created with **Pending** status. You will need to manage the account further to activate it.

#### Managing an individual user

Click **Manage** next to any user to open their detail page. Here you can (with `admin:users.write`):

- Change their **name**, **account type**, and **status** (Active, Pending, or Suspended)
- Assign or remove **roles**
- Assign or remove **team memberships**
- Add or remove **telephone numbers** on their behalf
- Send a **password reset link** to their email
- **Delete** the account (this cannot be undone)

> **Note:** Administrators cannot delete their own account from the admin panel, and the root account cannot be deleted at all. To delete your own account, use the [Danger Zone](#danger-zone--deleting-your-account) on your Profile page.

> **Note:** Users with `admin:users.read` but not `admin:users.write` can view user details but cannot make any changes.

### Roles and Permissions

**Required permission:** `admin:roles.read` (to view), `admin:roles.write` (to make changes)

Access via **Admin → Roles**.

The system uses a **capability-based permissions model**:

- A **capability** is a specific permission, such as the ability to view all users or to manage announcements.
- A **role** is a named collection of capabilities.
- Each user can be assigned one or more roles. They inherit all capabilities from all their roles.

#### Built-in roles

Two roles are created automatically when the system is first set up:

| Role | Who it is for | What it allows |
|---|---|---|
| **Root** | The lead administrator | Everything — full access to all features |
| **Volunteer** | Standard volunteers | Basic signed-in access |

#### Creating a new role

1. Enter a **name** and optionally a **description** for the new role.
2. Click **Create role**.
3. The new role appears in the list. Click on it to add capabilities.

#### Adding or removing capabilities from a role

On the role detail page, you will see all available capabilities. Capabilities already assigned to the role are highlighted. Click **Add** or **Remove** next to each one to change the role's permissions. Changes take effect immediately for any user who holds that role.

#### Available capabilities

| Capability | What it allows |
|---|---|
| `admin:users.read` | View all users |
| `admin:users.write` | Create, update, and delete users |
| `admin:roles.read` | View roles and capabilities |
| `admin:roles.write` | Create, update, and delete roles |
| `admin:teams.read` | View teams in the admin panel |
| `admin:teams.write` | Create, update, and delete teams and tasks |
| `admin:tasks.write` | Create and manage team task forms |
| `admin:audit.read` | View the audit log |
| `admin:files.read` | View and download uploaded files |
| `admin:files.write` | Upload and delete files |
| `admin:announcements.write` | Create, pin, and delete announcements |
| `admin:calendar.write` | Create and manage calendar events and jobs |
| `admin:theme.write` | Manage site content (e.g. privacy policy) |
| `admin:training.write` | Create and manage training policies |

### Team Management

**Required permission:** `admin:teams.read` (to view), `admin:teams.write` (to make changes)

Access via **Admin → Teams**.

Teams group volunteers together for organisational purposes. Each team can have a designated leader, a list of members, and a set of active tasks.

#### Creating a team

1. Enter a **team name** and an optional **description**.
2. Click **Create team**.

#### Managing a team

From the Teams admin page, each team card shows the team's name, description, number of members, and number of tasks. You can:

- **Edit** the name and description.
- **Set the team leader** — choose from the team's current members using the dropdown.
- **Delete** the team (only possible if the team has no members).

#### Team task forms

Team tasks define structured pieces of work for a team to carry out — for example, a maintenance inspection or a restoration job. Click **Manage task forms** to:

- Create a new task form with a title, description, task type, urgency level, personnel requirements, equipment list, and safety notes.
- Edit or delete existing task forms.

Active tasks created from these forms appear on the [Teams page](#10-teams) visible to all volunteers.

### Training Policies

**Required permission:** `admin:training.write`

Access via **Admin → Training**.

The Training page lets you define training requirements for different categories of volunteer and staff. These appear as a compliance matrix showing which account types (Volunteer, Staff, or Member) are required to complete each training item.

#### Adding a training policy

1. Enter the **policy title** (for example, "Fire Safety Awareness").
2. Enter the **frequency** — how often this training must be renewed (for example, "Annual" or "Every 3 years").
3. Add an optional **description**.
4. Tick the **account types** that this policy applies to.
5. Click **Add policy**.

#### Editing or deactivating a policy

Click **Edit** next to any policy to update its details or mark it as **Inactive**. Inactive policies remain on record but are no longer shown as current requirements.

### Schedule Management

**Required permission:** `admin:calendar.write`

Access via **Admin → Schedule**.

The admin schedule view looks similar to the volunteer calendar but gives administrators additional controls.

#### Creating a calendar event

Click on any day in the calendar to open the day detail panel, then fill in the **Create event** form:

- **Title** — a short name for the event or shift
- **Description** — optional details
- **Type** — choose from: Event, Roster slot (a shift that needs volunteers), or Help needed
- **Start and end time** — optional; leave blank for an all-day entry
- **Linked job** — optionally link this event to one of the defined jobs
- **Maximum sign-ups** — leave blank for unlimited

Click **Create event**. The event appears on the calendar immediately.

#### Deleting an event

In the day detail panel, each event has a **Delete** button. Deleting an event also removes all volunteer sign-ups for it.

#### Volunteer availability

Access via **Admin → Availability**.

This page shows a month-by-month view of which volunteers have recorded their availability. Click on any day in the calendar to see a detailed list of who is available, their time window, the duties they can help with, and any notes they have added. There is also a full-month table view at the bottom of the page.

#### Job management

Access via **Admin → Schedule → Jobs**.

Jobs are the duties that can appear on the calendar (for example, "Aircraft Guide" or "Tearoom Helper"). Each job can be either a **rolling duty** (always available to sign up for) or a **rostered duty** (appears on specific scheduled events).

From this page you can:
- **Create** a new job with a title, description, type (rolling or rostered), and colour.
- **Edit** existing jobs.
- **Delete** jobs that are no longer needed.

**Required permission:** `admin:announcements.write`

Access via **Admin → Announcements**.

From this page you can:

- **Create** a new announcement by entering a title and body text, then clicking **Post announcement**.
- **Pin** an announcement to keep it at the top of the list for all users — pinned announcements are highlighted in amber. Click **Unpin** to remove the pin.
- **Delete** an announcement — this removes it permanently.

All announcements appear on the [Announcements page](#8-announcements) visible to signed-in users, and a preview is shown on the Dashboard.

### File Management

**Required permission:** `admin:files.read` (to view), `admin:files.write` (to delete)

Access via **Admin → Files**.

This page shows all uploaded files with their original name, type, size, uploader, and upload date. You can:

- **Download** any file.
- **Delete** a file — this removes it from the server permanently.

To upload new files, use the Upload page (see [Section 15](#15-uploading-files-administrators)).

### Audit Log

**Required permission:** `admin:audit.read`

Access via **Admin → Audit log**.

The audit log is a complete, permanent record of every significant action in the system. It cannot be edited or deleted.

Each entry shows:
- **When** — the date and time of the action (UK format: day/month/year, 24-hour clock)
- **User** — who performed the action (or "system" for automatic actions)
- **Action** — a code describing what happened (for example, `USER_SIGNIN`, `USER_CREATED`, `FILE_UPLOADED`, `ANNOUNCEMENT_CREATED`)
- **Resource** — what type of record was affected
- **Details** — any additional information recorded at the time

The log is paginated (50 entries per page). You can filter by:
- **Action keyword** — for example, type `USER` to see only user-related events
- **User email or name** — to see only actions by a specific person

### Site Content

**Required permission:** `admin:theme.write`

Access via **Admin → Site content**.

This page lets you edit the text of the **Privacy & Cookie Policy** displayed on the public `/privacy` page. The policy is stored in the database and can be updated at any time.

> **Important:** The default policy was drafted with the assistance of AI and has not been formally reviewed by a legal professional. You should have the policy reviewed and customised for the museum before relying on it.

Edit the text in the box and click **Save**. A **Preview** link lets you see how the policy will appear to visitors.

## 17. Email Notifications

The VMS sends email notifications automatically in certain situations:

|---|---|

| **Password reset link** | When you request a password reset |
| **Admin password reset** | When an administrator sends a reset link on your behalf |

All emails come from the museum's VMS email address and will have "CNAM VMS" in the sender name. If you are not receiving emails, check your junk or spam folder and, if necessary, add the sender address to your contacts.

## 18. Privacy and Security

The VMS is designed with privacy and security in mind:

- **Passwords are stored securely.** Your password is stored as a one-way cryptographic hash (scrypt). No one — including administrators — can read your password. It can only be reset, never retrieved.
- **Two-step sign-in.** As well as your password, a one-time code is sent to your email each time you sign in. This means even if someone else obtained your password, they could not sign in without also having access to your email.
- **Your email address is kept private.** It is only visible to administrators and is stored securely.
- **Everything is logged.** Every sign-in and every significant action is recorded in the audit log, so any unusual activity can be spotted quickly.
- **Access is controlled and hidden.** Each person can only see and do what they have been given permission for. Pages you do not have access to are not shown — they redirect silently to your dashboard rather than displaying an "Access Denied" message that would confirm the page exists.
- **Suspended accounts are blocked immediately.** If your account is suspended, you are redirected to an error page on your very next page load.
- **File uploads are checked.** Only certain types of file can be uploaded, and the system checks both the file's extension and its actual content type to prevent unsafe files from being stored.
- **The system is kept up to date.** Security updates to the underlying software are applied regularly.

If you notice anything unusual or suspect that your account has been accessed without your knowledge, please contact your administrator immediately.

## 19. Frequently Asked Questions

**I entered my email and password but the verification code never arrived. What do I do?**

First, check your junk or spam folder — these emails are sometimes filtered there. If it is not there after five minutes, go back to the sign-in page and try again. Make sure you are using the email address registered with your account. If problems continue, contact your administrator.

---

**The verification code I received says it is invalid or expired. What do I do?**

Verification codes expire after 10 minutes. Go back to the sign-in page, enter your email and password again, and a new code will be sent. If you enter the wrong code five times, the code is cancelled for security — start the sign-in process again.

---

**I have forgotten my password. What do I do?**

Click **Forgot password?** on the sign-in page. Enter your email address and a reset link will be sent to you. Follow the link and set a new password. The link expires after 24 hours. If you have any problems, contact your administrator.

---

**I can sign in but it says my account is "Pending". What does that mean?**

Your account has been created but has not yet been approved by an administrator. Contact your team leader or museum administrator and ask them to activate your account.

---

**It says "Access Denied" when I try to open a page. What do I do?**

This means you do not have permission to view that page. If you believe you should have access, contact your administrator and let them know which page you were trying to reach.

---

**How do I change my name or telephone number?**

Go to your **Profile** page (click your name in the top-right corner). From there you can update your display name and manage your telephone numbers directly.

---

**How do I change my email address?**

Email addresses can only be changed by an administrator. Contact your administrator with your new email address and they will update it for you.

---

**Can I use the VMS on my phone?**

Yes. The VMS works in the web browser on any smartphone or tablet. Open your phone's browser, go to the VMS address, and sign in as normal.

---

**I have forgotten the web address for the VMS. Where can I find it?**

Your administrator will be able to give you the address. It will usually start with `https://` followed by the museum's domain name.

---

**Can I stay signed in permanently?**

Tick the **"Keep me signed in for 7 days"** checkbox on the sign-in page. Your session will last seven days before you need to sign in again. Do not use this option on a shared or public computer. Without the checkbox, your session expires after two hours of inactivity.

---

**Something is broken or not working as I expect. Who do I tell?**

Please contact your administrator and describe what you were doing, what you expected to happen, and what actually happened. If you see any error messages, note them down or take a screenshot — this will help the administrator (or the technical team) to fix the problem more quickly.
